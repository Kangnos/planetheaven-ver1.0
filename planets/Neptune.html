What is Neptune?

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System.
Neptune is 17 times the mass of Earth, slightly mroe massive than is near-twin Uranus. Neptune is
denser and physically smaller than Uranus because its greater mass causes more gravitational compression of its atmosphere.
Neptune orbits the Sun once every 164.8 years at an average distance of 30.1 au(4.5 billion km; 2.8 billion mi). It named after the Roman god of the sea.
